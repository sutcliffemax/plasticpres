Who Is Bottling Plastic Pollution?

Plastic pollution harms the environment, wildlife, human health, and incurs significant economic costs. This project aims to explore global trends in plastic pollution and identify the countries that generate, recycle, and mismanage the most plastic. To tackle plastic pollution, we have to know where it is coming from. Only then can we raise awareness of the issue and guide effective strategies for reducing it.

Conclusion

Rapidly increasing production of plastic products is overwhelming the world's ability to manage it. Although high-income countries generate much more plastic, they pollute far less because they tend to have more effective waste management systems. Further research is essential to find the most effective policies to reduce plastic pollution, to start they should be targeted towards helping low-to-middle-income countries improve their waste management and regulations. Increasing awareness of plastic pollution and discouraging the use of single-use plastic will also play a vital role.
