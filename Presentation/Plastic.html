Quality Control







One of the biggest challenges is assessing the true quality of recyclable materials

Why is Quality Control such a big deal?

🍋 Market for "Lemons" Concept: 🍋

- George Akerlof's Nobel Prize winning economic theory regarding asymmetric information in markets.

- Refers to the challenge of distinguishing between high-quality and low-quality products.

♻ Recycling Industry's Dilemma: ♻

- Absence of accurate quality assessment in the recycling market.

- Difficulty in differentiating between good and bad suppliers.

💰 Impact on Buyers: 💰

- Buyers cannot discern between quality levels.

- Reluctance to pay fair prices due to uncertainty.

⚠️ Consequences for the Industry: ⚠️

- Reduction in supply of high-quality recycled materials.

- Imbalance in the market, leading to collapse in recyclables being traded.







The Solution: Quality Control with AI

Incorporating computer vision and machine learning technologies into recycling facilities revolutionises quality control processes.

Quality Control: AI systems can monitor the quality of recycled materials by analyzing data from sensors and cameras throughout the recycling process.


Sellers can leverage AI-generated data to prove the quality of their recycled materials, enabling them to command better prices in the market.


Buyers, in turn, gain confidence in the reliability and consistency of recycled waste, leading to increased demand and increased trade in recyclables.
